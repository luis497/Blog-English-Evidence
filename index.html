Crime and Punishment

Learning Goal: Encourage a reflective understanding of justice, focusing on how crime and punishment affect both individuals and society, and exploring different approaches to ethical decision-making. Main Themes and Activities 1. Understanding Crime and Its Social Implications Overview: This section dives into the social context of crime, discussing root causes, social impact, and the societal responses. It aims to help students analyze crime beyond just statistics, encouraging them to think about underlying factors and the role of society in both prevention and response. Learning Activities: Community Impact Map: Students create a visual map illustrating the impact of crime on different community groups (e.g., families, schools, businesses) to identify the broader social consequences. Case Comparison Exercise: Students compare two case studies of similar crimes in different social contexts, discussing how factors like poverty, education, and community support shape outcomes. Skills Developed: Critical thinking about the causes and effects of crime and awareness of social factors influencing criminal behavior.
Critical Thinking and Media Literacy

Learning Goal: Equip students with skills to critically evaluate media content, recognize bias, and make informed decisions. This topic will also foster awareness of how media influences perceptions and behaviors in society. Main Themes and Activities 1. Understanding Media Influence on Society Overview: Focuses on the role of media in shaping public opinion, influencing trends, and setting cultural narratives. Students learn to recognize different forms of media influence, including advertisements, news, and social media. Learning Activities: Media Content Analysis Workshop: Students bring in examples of media content (ads, news articles, social media posts) and analyze the intended messages, the persuasive techniques used, and potential biases. Influence Map Creation: Students create a map showing how different media sources influence their own daily lives and perceptions, fostering self-awareness of media impact. Skills Developed: Recognition of media’s role in shaping opinions and understanding of various media techniques used to influence audiences.
